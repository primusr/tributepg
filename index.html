Everything to Know About Them.

History

  • Pre-debut
    The band's promotional preparation began in November 2011 with the revelation of an teaser image of Kim Eun-bi, who was speculated as a candidate for the group's line-up.

  • 2016: Debut, "Square One", "Square Two"
    On June 29, 2016, YG Entertainment revealed the final line-up and name of the group. BLACKPINK is the first girl group to debut under YG Entertainment since 2NE1's debut in 2009. The group debuted on August 8 with their first digital single album, "Square One". The single album has two songs, "Whistle" and "Boombayah" which are the title tracks for the album.

    BLACKPINK released their second digital single album, "Square Two" on November 1 with "Playing With Fire" and "Stay" serving as the title tracks. Both songs were produced by Teddy, R.Tee, and Seo Won Jin. "Playing With Fire" debuted at #1 on the Billboard World Digital Songs chart, making it their second song to do so.

  • 2017: Japan Debut, "As If It's Your Last"
    On May 17, it was announced that the group would be debuting in Japan with their debut album which was released on August 9. It was also revealed that they would be holding their debut showcase on July 20 at Budokan in Chiyoda, Tokyo, Japan.

    On July 17, an article was posted on the YGEX website announcing that BLACKPINK would be releasing their debut Japanese mini album, Blackpink, on August 9. The music videos for the Japanese versions of "Boombayah" and "Whistle were released on August 30.

  • 2018: Square Up, 2018 Tour (In Your Area) Seoul x BC Card, World Tour
    On June 1, 2018, a moving poster was posted on the group's social media accounts announcing that the group would be releasing their first mini album, Square Up, on June 15. "Ddu-Du Ddu-Du" served as the album's title track. The song debuted at #2 for the month of June on the monthly Gaon Chart. It then peaked at #1 the following month. "Ddu-Du Ddu-Du" also debuted at #55 on the Billboard Hot 100, breaking the previous record held by "Nobody" by the Wonder Girls and became the highest highest-charting song by a Korean girl group.

    On September 12, it was announced that BLACKPINK would be having their first solo concert, BLACKPINK 2018 Tour (In Your Area) Seoul x BC Card, in Seoul, South Korea the Olympic Gymnastics Arena. YG Entertainment released a statement on October 23, stating that BLACKPINK has signed with an American label, Interscope Records.

    On November 1, it was revealed that the group would be having their first world tour, BLACKPINK 2019 World Tour (In Your Area). It began on January 11 in Bangkok, Thailand at the IMPACT Arena.

  • 2019: Kill This Love, Coachella
    On February 9, 2019, BLACKPINK performed at the CEO of Universal Music Group's Showcase party. This was their first US performance. On February 11, the group performed at The Late Show, making it the first US television appearance . They performed at Good Morning America the following day. BLACKPINK was the on the cover for Billboard's magazine for the month of March.

    BLACKPINK released their third mini album, Kill This Love, with the same name title track on April 5.

    On April 12 and April 19, BLACKPINK performed at Coachella, which made them the first Kpop girl group to perform there.

  • 2020: "Sour Candy", "How You Like That", "Ice Cream", The Album
    In April 2020, Lady Gaga revealed that she would be releasing "Sour Candy" which is a collaboration with BLACKPINK. A lyric video for the song was posted on June 16. The song debuted at #33 on the Billboard Hot 100.

    In June, YG revealed that BLACKPINK would have a three-part comeback with two pre-release singles and the album to end it. The first pre-release, "How You Like That", was released on June 26.

    On July 23, a teaser poster was posted which teased that the group would be releasing a collaboration single by an unnamed artist in August On July 28, a teaser poster revealed that BLACKPINK would be releasing their first album, The Album, on October 2.

    August 11, Selena Gomez was revealed to be the unnamed artist. The song was revealed to be titled, "Ice Cream" two days later. "Ice Cream" was released on August 28.

  • 2021: BLACKPINK: The Movie, 4+1 Project
    On June 16, 2021, it was announced that BLACKPINK: The Movie will air on August 8, as part of their "4+1 PROJECT" to commemorate the group's fifth anniversary. On July 14, YG announced that BLACKPINK would be joining WEVERSE on August 2, as part of the “4+1 Project”. On July 28, YG announced BLACKPINK’s 2021 Summer Diary as part of the “4+1 Project," it is set to be released on August 25. To celebrate their 5th anniversary, a BLACKPINK island, ‘In Your Area Island’, will be released on Animal Crossing: New Horizons on August 6. As the final part of the "4+1 PROJECT," a special pop up exhibition will be opening on August 21 until August 30. The exhibition will reflect the concept of the songs BLACKPINK has released so far.

  • 2022: BLACKPINK: The Virtual, "Pink Venom" and "Born Pink"
    On July 26, 2022, YG Entertainment stated that BLACKPINK are in the middle of filming a music video and added that it's the most highest production cost they have ever spent on an MV.

    On August 1, 2022, a Born Pink announcement trailer was posted on the group's social media accounts announcing that the group will be releasing a pre-release single in August, an album in September, and starting a world tour in October. Later that day, YG explained that Born Pink is the project name for their upcoming releases. On August 8, a teaser poster was posted which revealed that the group will be releasing the pre-release single, "Pink Venom" on August 19. The next day, a schedule announcement was posted which announced that the world tour, BLACKPINK World Tour 'Born Pink', will start on October 15 at Seoul, South Korea. On August 10, a teaser poster was posted which revealed that the group will be releasing their second studio album, Born Pink on September 16.

    On September 16, Born Pink and its title track "Shut Down" were both released.

    BLACKPINK was named the 2022 Entertainer Of The Year by TIME Magazine which makes them the first girl group to receive the title of Entertainer Of The Year.
